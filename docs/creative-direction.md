# Creative Direction — Adrián Álvarez Personal Website

*This document captures the design vision, positioning, and aesthetic rationale
for the project. It is the "why" behind every decision in CLAUDE.md.*

---

## The central idea

Most personal websites make the same mistake: they try to look like more than they are.
They dress up a CV as a product, or dress up ambition as achievement.

This site does the opposite.

The design itself is the argument. The quality of what is built — the typography,
the spacing, the writing, the restraint — communicates more than any list of credentials.
A visitor should feel: "This person thinks carefully." And then they should want to
read more.

Adrián is at the beginning of his career, and he is honest about that. That honesty,
expressed through a site that genuinely earns its premium feel rather than faking it,
is rarer and more compelling than overclaiming would ever be.

---

## Positioning

**Not:** "Here's everything I've done."  
**Instead:** "Here's how I see the world — and if that resonates, let's talk."

The site is an honest introduction, not a pitch. It is a digital home, not a brochure.

The visitor Adrián wants to reach — a founder, investor, researcher, builder, economist —
has a highly calibrated sense of authenticity. They can tell when a site is performing.
This site should not perform. It should simply be.

---

## What makes the references feel premium (and how we apply it)

Adrián cited Apple, Linear, Vercel, and Javier Sánchez. Here is what they share:

| Principle | How it manifests |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Confidence through restraint** | They show less than they could. They resist over-explaining. |
| **Typography as design** | Font choice, size, weight, and spacing ARE the design — not containers for it. |
| **Spatial rhythm** | Consistent, generous whitespace signals that each element was placed intentionally. |
| **Motion with purpose** | Transitions are subtle, fast, functional. Never decorative. |
| **Color discipline** | Near-neutral palettes. One carefully placed accent. Nothing competing. |
| **Voice consistency** | The copy and the design feel like they came from the same person. |
| **Invisible details** | The things you notice on second look — hover states, spacing decisions, line lengths. |

The goal is not to copy these. It is to apply these principles in a way that
reflects Adrián's specific personality: analytical, curious, calm, detail-oriented.

## Visual language

### Typography

**Primary:** Geist Variable (self-hosted, Vercel open source)

Chosen because:
- Clean, precise, and contemporary without being trendy
- Excellent at both display sizes and body text
- Consistent with the Apple/Linear/Vercel reference space
- Variable font — one file, full range of weights
- Not a commodity choice (not the first thing a developer reaches for)

**Accent serif (occasional use only):**
A refined serif used sparingly — pull quotes, editorial openers, special moments.
Never part of the core visual identity. Typeface to be finalized once primary system
is established.

### Color palette

Near-neutral throughout. Nothing competes for attention.

The background is not pure white — it is a warm, slightly off-white (`#F9F9F7`)
that reads like good paper. This small decision makes a visible difference: it is
softer on the eye and signals more care than a default white.

Dark mode uses the same logic: not pure black (`#0E0E0D`), slightly warm.

The accent color is intentionally undecided at this stage. It should be muted and
desaturated — a shade more than a color. It will be chosen once the typographic
system is in place, so it can be evaluated in actual context rather than in isolation.

### Spacing

Generous spacing is a design decision, not a consequence of having little content.
It signals confidence. It slows the visitor down in a good way.

The reference here is book design, not web app design. A well-designed book has
breathing room. The margins are not wasted space — they are part of the composition.

### Motion

Three rules that govern every animation decision:

1. Does it serve the content?
2. Would removing it make the experience worse?
3. Is it as subtle as it can be while still working?

If the answer to 1 or 2 is no: remove it.
If the answer to 3 is no: reduce it.

Specific constraints: no scroll-triggered element movement, no parallax, no
loading screens. Permitted: page fade-in, opacity transitions, small hover state shifts.

## Page-by-page rationale

### Home (`/`)

Not a hero. Not a tagline. Not a call to action.

Something quieter: a name, a single sentence that places Adrián without overselling him,
and then entry points to the rest of the site. Think of it like the title page of a book.

The home page should answer one question — "Who is this?" — and make the visitor
want to go deeper. It should not answer every question at once.

### About (`/about`)

This is the most important page on the site.

Not a bio. Not a timeline. A personal essay — the kind a thoughtful person would write,
not the kind a PR team would approve.

It should cover:
- Who Adrián is and how he got here
- What he cares about and why
- How he thinks about the intersection of technology, economics, and AI
- Where he is now and where he is going (honestly — not inflated)

The career narrative (academic work, AI startup, Amsterdam) lives here as prose,
integrated into the essay rather than structured as a timeline. A dedicated
experience page can be added later when there is more to say.

### Writing (`/writing`)

Not a blog. The word "blog" implies frequency and informality that doesn't match
the quality standard here.

Essays and notes. Differentiated by length and weight. Published only when
Adrián would stand behind them indefinitely.

The writing section will start with very little — perhaps one piece — and that
is correct. One genuinely good piece of thinking is worth more than ten rushed ones.

### Contact (`/contact`)

The simplest page on the site. An email address. The social links Adrián actually uses.

Nothing else. No contact form. No "let's connect" language. Just the honest,
direct way to reach him.

## Voice

The copy on this site should sound like Adrián talking to a peer — someone he
respects and can speak plainly with.

**Clear rather than clever.** Avoid wordplay, puns, and clever constructions.
They draw attention to themselves rather than to the idea.

**Confident but not arrogant.** State things directly. Don't hedge. But don't
overclaim either — especially on things Adrián is still learning.

**Professional without being corporate.** "Professional" means precise and
reliable, not formal or stiff.

**Honest about where he is.** The site should not read like Adrián has already
arrived. It should read like someone who knows exactly where he is and where
he is going, and is not embarrassed by the distance between them.

---

## What this site is not

- It is not a portfolio
- It is not a resume
- It is not a startup landing page
- It is not a blog
- It is not a personal brand

It is a place where someone can go to understand who Adrián Álvarez is —
and decide whether they want to work with him, think alongside him, or follow
his work as it develops.

That is enough. That is the goal.
